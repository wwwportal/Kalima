Book of Relevant Invocations
ربي ثبت فؤادي على حكمك
My lord, steady my perception upon your linguistic discernment.

My lord, fill my heart with peace, joy, and gratitude. Guide me to find insight in every experience and help me let go of worries that cloud my spirit. Grant me the memory to remain hopeful and patient, and allow my thoughts to sear me upon the path of your guidance.

My lord, enable me to perceive the symbolic associations between objects in the Quran and objects in the world.

My Lord, rekindle in me my memories of Your gifts, for gratitude is naught but remembering the good I’ve received. Therefore I need to prepare my farm to receive the divine rain, that I may communicate with you using the mature fruits of your seeds, that gratitude may flow from my tongue. Grant me the seeds of knowledge I once tasted - those precious clouds I had taken shelter from in my ignorance in the way of land cultivation. My Lord, I yearn to reconnect with You, to feel Your presence, in the form of abstract understanding combined with wonderful emotions. My Lord, in Your infinite wisdom, you made me a creature with gradual development and proportioned volition, perchance I may ask to learn about the unseen realms, and their relationship to the earthly realm. Perfect as you are, you created me imperfect, and peace with one's imperfections is granted to those who do not associate with Allah. Lead me to the pathway of serenity and discipline. Every time an evil within myself shows itself to me is an opportunity to be grateful to my Lord for enabling me to see my mistakes, for it brings me one step closer to firdaous.

My lord, my master, my teacher, my caretaker, my cultivator. I used to think that you like to hear your own praises. And that you'd get offended by me saying such a thing. Of course not. Me praising you is me remembering reality. Through active recall, my knowledge of reality gets reinforced, and the seeds may continue grow to fruition. My lord, you are self-sufficient, without need for any of our praises and prayers, you
